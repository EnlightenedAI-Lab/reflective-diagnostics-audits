What this is

Reflective Diagnostics is a modular audit framework for evaluating not only what a model outputs, but how stable it remains, how well its claims are supported, how it behaves under pressure, and how it fails on exact-answer tasks.

What it measures

  • Structural support and reasoning quality
  • Revision behavior and drift
  • Uncertainty and semantic fragmentation
  • Confidence versus evidence density
  • Constraint grounding and integrity
  • Boundary hold, snap, and recovery under pressure

Why it matters

  • Format-compliant but wrong
  • Correct but structurally unstable
  • High-confidence hallucination
  • Low-confidence failure
  • Semantic fragmentation across runs
  • Different failure styles hidden by ordinary benchmarks
