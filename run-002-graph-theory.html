What was tested

Five frontier models were tested on dense graph shortest-path tasks. Each item required the exact shortest path distance between two nodes.

This audit was designed to measure more than final accuracy. It also measured whether models stayed structured, became overconfident, or failed under relational load.

Key result Large context does not guarantee strong relational reasoning. This run exposed sharp differences between accuracy, formatting stability, and confidence behavior.
Why this matters
A model can look stable and still be confidently wrong.
Run scope
5 models · 20 graph items each · 100 total records.
Audit advantage
This separates correctness, structure, and confidence instead of collapsing everything into one score.

Run setup

  • Module: L15 Battery Runner
  • Benchmark family: Graph Theory Oracle
  • Models: GPT-5.2, Claude Opus 4.6, Gemini 2.5 Flash, DeepSeek Chat, Grok-3
  • Batch size: 20 graph items per model
  • Total records: 100

Overall interpretation

This was a difficult batch, but not a total collapse. The run clearly separated different model failure styles.

  • Claude Opus 4.6 led accuracy.
  • GPT-5.2 stayed perfectly formatted but was highly overconfident when wrong.
  • Gemini 2.5 Flash showed severe parse weakness and provider errors.
  • Grok-3 was the most overconfident lane.
  • DeepSeek Chat had the weakest overall profile.

Notable failure patterns

Confidently wrong

GPT-5.2 and Grok-3 both preserved format, but their confidence behavior made wrong answers more dangerous rather than less.

Signal: strong structure does not equal trustworthy reasoning.

Cautious but incomplete

Gemini 2.5 Flash showed low arrogance, but the lane suffered from parse failures and provider errors.

Signal: caution alone does not create reliable completion.

Accuracy without structural resilience

Claude Opus 4.6 led the batch on accuracy, yet still showed 60.0% structural decay.

Signal: better answers do not automatically mean stable behavior under load.

Full breakdown profile

DeepSeek Chat combined the lowest accuracy with complete structural decay and a very high arrogance score.

Signal: this was the clearest weak profile in the batch.

Why this is better than a simple benchmark

A normal benchmark would mostly reduce this run to a score. This audit preserves more useful evidence: who was correct, who stayed stable, who broke format, who became overconfident, and who failed cautiously.

Bottom line Dense graph reasoning remains a useful stress test for frontier models, especially when correctness, structure, and confidence are measured separately.
